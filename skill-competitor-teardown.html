Competitor Teardown

Give it one competitor — a domain or a name — and get back a sharp, opinionated battlecard built entirely from Bright Data. It reads what they're building from open job listings, what their customers hate from reviews, and how they sell themselves today from posts and their site. Five sections: Snapshot, Where they're investing, Current messaging, The cracks, Win themes.

Why use it

A battlecard tells sales and marketing three things they pay analysts to dig up: what a competitor is building, what their customers hate, and how they position themselves right now. All three sit in public data the competitor can't hide — it just never gets read together. This skill reads it together, from a single input, using only the Bright Data MCP.

The two non-obvious moves that make it sharp:

Open roles = roadmap. A company's job listings leak its priorities before any announcement. 12 of 18 open reqs on the data-platform team → they're rebuilding the core. 6 new enterprise AEs → they're moving upmarket. Read the hiring, predict the next two quarters.

Negative reviews = your wedge. The pattern in 1–3★ reviews is the durable weakness — the thing they can't fix fast. That's where you position.

How to use it

STEP 1 — INSTALL THE SKILL

Click Download Skill above. You get a competitor-teardown.md file. In Claude, open Settings → Capabilities → Skills and upload the file. It's now available across all your conversations.

STEP 2 — CONNECT BRIGHT DATA MCP

This skill's only dependency is Bright Data — no other tools. In Claude, go to Settings → Connectors → Add custom connector and paste this URL (replace YOUR_API_TOKEN with your Bright Data token):

https://mcp.brightdata.com/sse?token=YOUR_API_TOKEN

One-time setup. Get your token at brightdata.com/cp/setting/users.

STEP 3 — TRIGGER THE SKILL

In any conversation, just say it naturally:

  • "Tear down stripe.com"
  • "Battlecard for Clay"
  • "What is Apollo building?"
  • "What do Outreach's customers hate?"
  • "How do I beat Instantly?" — or just /teardown

Add a lens to pre-aim the wedge: "tear down Apollo from the POV of my product". Save your one-line offer once and every teardown frames the win themes against it.

STEP 4 — READ THE BATTLECARD

Claude returns a five-section card, stamped with the date it was checked. It's the deliverable — nothing gets enriched or pushed:

Teardown — Acme Data
built from public data · checked 2026-06-28
1. Snapshot ▲

Series B, $40M (Mar 2025, led by Acme Ventures). ~180 employees, headcount +22% in 6 months. A data-pipeline platform aimed at mid-market eng teams. Ascending.

2. Where they're investing → roadmap

14 of 21 open reqs are eng on the ingestion/AI side → they're rebuilding the data layer; expect feature parity on transforms within ~2 quarters. 4 enterprise AE reqs → moving upmarket. Zero support reqs → the support gap below won't close soon.

3. How they sell themselves now

Leading with "AI-native pipelines, zero config." Proof = logos + a 5-min setup demo. Targeting eng leaders now, not the analysts they courted last year.

4. The cracks → your opening

~60% of negative reviews hit onboarding ("took 3 weeks to go live"). ~30% cite slow support. Onboarding is structural (no support hiring) → that's the wedge.

5. Win themes

① "Live in a day, not a month" — aim at the onboarding crack. ② Named human support vs. their ticket queue. ③ Analyst-friendly, the segment they're walking away from.

What it reads (Bright Data only)

  • Snapshot → Crunchbase company + LinkedIn company profile (size, funding, trajectory)
  • Roadmap → LinkedIn job listings — bucketed by function, counted, tech in descriptions extracted
  • Messaging → recent LinkedIn posts + their homepage / product pages scraped to markdown
  • The cracks → 1–3★ review verbatims from G2 / Capterra / Trustpilot, Google Maps, or Amazon — whichever fits the category
  • Momentum → recent news + a fresh search pass

Good to know

  • Recency is the point. Jobs and news use a 30-day window, messaging 45 days, funding 180. Anything older is labeled "as of <date> — may have shifted", never passed off as current.
  • Track over time. Save a competitor and ask to "re-run my teardowns" — it diffs against last time (new roles, changed messaging, new complaints, funding moves). The diff is often more interesting than the snapshot.
  • Honest about thin data. Stealth company or few reviews → it widens the search and says the section is light. It never invents complaints or roadmap items.
  • A hiring freeze is a signal too — no open roles reads as stalling / cost-cutting, not a blank section.

What this skill does NOT do

  • Use any MCP other than Bright Data — it's fully self-contained
  • Enrich or contact anyone — the battlecard is the deliverable, there's no lead step
  • Present stale data as current — out-of-window sections are labeled, not hidden
  • Hedge — a battlecard that won't name the wedge isn't worth making
  • Invent complaints or roadmap items — every claim traces to a real Bright Data pull
